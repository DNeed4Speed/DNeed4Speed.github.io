Check - In: Introduction

In TOM Team, Check - In is by far the most complicated process to learn and even more importantly, do correctly. It requires the Associate to memorize several pieces of information and navigate multiple applications within a short period of time.

Important:

Drivers must

  • Wear a Class 2 Vest

  • Drive 5 MPH in the Yard

  • Stay off Phones while driving

Never Check In a Pup as

  • 53 Foot Trailer

  • Intermodal Trailer

Check - In: Procedure

- Starting a Check - In:

  • Check Virtually and Physically the Tractor and Trailer match (this should automatically happen on GEM)
  • Verify the Tractor License Plate and Trailer ID, using the cameras, to see that the virtual information fully matches what is physically at Check - In
  • Once the information has been verified, ask the driver either on the Phone or Directly for their VRID / Load ID / BOL (You can say: "What is the LAST 4 of your Load ID / BOL?")

    • Note: Always verify whether the Driver is Picking Up a second trailer or Bobtailing out of the yard, even when GEM does not generate a VRID or give any other indication

  • After the Tractor, Trailer and VRID have been verified to be a Full Match, you can now Check In the Driver on GEM by clicking the Check In button

Check - In: Virtual Procedure

- Starting a Check - In using GEM manually:

  • Sometimes, you will have to enter the Tractor License Plate and Trailer ID manually - GEM is not perfect and will not always enter all the information automatically

    • Note: The same goes for the VRID

  • When a Driver arrives with a Trailer, add the Trailer by selecting Add Equipment - then select No License Plate and enter the Trailer ID in full

    • Note: Do this after verifying the Tractor License Plate - Use the cameras

  • Add the VRID by selecting Add Appointment on the Bottom-Right portion of the screen
  • To be safe: Enter this information into Slack after you're done - better to keep a record of a Driver that was not automatically recognized by GEM

Check - In: Problem Solving

- Check - In; Encountering errors:

  • When a VRID fails to generate automatically by GEM, the Associate needs to add the appointment manually
  • Click on Add Appointment and type the last four of the VRID in the search bar. If the VRID does not appear, type in the Full VRID
  • If the Tractor / Trailer does not match, you can change the Tractor License Plate, Trailer SCAC, Trailer Type, and Trailer ID using the Edit feature in GEM that is located next to Remove

    • Note: Sometimes Trailers are brought from the Railroad and arrive too early. This will also require a manual input of the full VRID. If the VRID still does not generate, place the VRID in FMC and locate the CR-ID
